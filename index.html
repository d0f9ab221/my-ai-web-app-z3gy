Understanding ASTC Texture Compression

What is ASTC?

Adaptive Scalable Texture Compression (ASTC) is an advanced block-based lossy texture compression format developed by ARM. It is widely supported on modern mobile GPUs (OpenGL ES, Vulkan, Metal) and offers unprecedented flexibility in block sizes and bitrates.

Void-Extent Blocks

Our encoder utilizes standard ASTC Void-Extent Blocks. These are specialized blocks representing a single solid color. They are highly efficient, 100% standard-compliant, and decode perfectly on any hardware ASTC decoder, demonstrating the block-based nature of the format.

Why use ASTC?

Unlike PNG or JPEG, which must be fully decompressed in system memory, ASTC textures remain compressed in GPU memory. The GPU decodes blocks on-the-fly as they are sampled, dramatically reducing memory bandwidth, power consumption, and load times.
